Prediction of 1-Bedroom House Prices in North Carolina


I. CHECK THE DISTRIBUTION OF HOUSE PRICES
  • House prices have a positive skew with higher concentration on cheaper homes.
  • To get a more symmetrical distribution, the logarithm of house prices could help.
    • Skew= 1.66
    • Kurtosis = 4.67
II. NORMALIZE DISTRIBUTION BY TAKING LOGARITHM OF HOUSE PRICES
  • Taking the logarithm of house prices, we obtain something similar to a Gaussian distribution.
  • This logarithmic distribution will ensure that relative errors for cheaper and more expensive homes are treated on an equal footing.
    • Skew (for Log.Dist.) = -0.48
    • Kurtosis (for Log.Dist.) = 0.65
III. MEASURE LINEAR RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN THE PREDICTOR VARIABLES
  1. An important point in selecting predictors for a linear regression model is to check for multicolinearity (if multiple predictors mean the same thing).
  2. Population and Number of Schools are strongly correlated to each other with a correlation co-efficient of 0.81. These feature pairs can affect the model.
  3. Similarly, the Median Household Income and Poverty Rate have a strong negative correlation of -0.72 which could also cause multicollinearity.
IV. BUILD THE LINEAR REGRESSION MODEL
  • Number of Samples: 406
  • Target Variable: Median House Price (USD)
  • Total Predictors: 6
    • Numerical Predictors: 4
    • Categorical Predictors: 2
  • Predictor Information:
    • City (Categorical)
    • Zipcode (Categorical)
    • Population per zipcode (Numerical)
    • Median Household Income per zipcode (Numerical)
    • Poverty Rate per zipcode (Numerical)
    • Number of Public Schoolsper zipcode (Numerical)
V. MEASURE THE PERFORMANCE OF THE PREDICTIVE MODEL
  • Training Score (r-square): 0.324
  • Testing Score (r-square): 0.244

*Residuals are the difference between the true house prices and the predicted house prices.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
  1. The prediction model needs improvement based on the performance measures shown above.
  2. r-square closer to 1 indicates a better model. This model has a score of 0.244.
  3. Predictive line cannot be plotted in a 3D space for the multiple Linear Regression model, so residuals were plotted instead.
  4. Residual plot shows that the predictive values are not very close to the zero on the y-axis indicating high residuals.
  5. Highly collinear factors (like Population, Median Household Income, Poverty Rate) could be negatively effecting scores.
